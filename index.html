Did you know?

There are 4 forms of spina bifida

Occulta: mildest and most common form in which one or more bones of the spinal column (vertebrae) are malformed.

Closed neural tube defects: a diverse group of disorders in which the spine may have malformations of fat, bone, or the membranes (the meninges) that cover the spinal cord.

Meningocele: occurs when the meninges protrude through the spine and cause a sac of spinal fluid on the back.

Myelomeningocele: the most severe form of spina bifida. A portion of the spinal cord or nerves are exposed in a sac through an opening in the spine.

The causes of spina bifida are unknown

Spina bifida happens in the first few weeks of pregnancy, often before a woman knows she's pregnant. Although folic acid is not a guarantee that a woman will have a healthy pregnancy, taking folic acid can help reduce a woman's risk of having a pregnancy affected by spina bifida.

There are indirect effects of spina bifida

Some individuals with spina bifida may face challenges related to weight management, either due to limited mobility or medication side effects. A personalized nutrition plan can address weight concerns and promote a healthy body composition.

Spina bifida can be difficult to notice at first glance.

Most children with spina bifida follow typical growth patterns until they are around four years of age; after that time, increased fat mass (versus lean mass) has been found when compared with children without spina bifida.
