What is Spina Bifida?

Spina bifida is an idiopathic congenital condition characterized by the inadequate formation of the spine. During the initial month of pregnancy in the case of spina bifida, the neural tube fails to fully seal, leading to the incomplete closure of certain spinal bones. This condition can give rise to openings along the spine, potentially causing harm to the spinal cord and nerves.

There are 4 forms of Spina Bifida. Occulta, the mildest and most prevalent form, is characterized by malformations in one or more vertebrae. Closed neural tube defects occur as the spine may exhibit malformations involving fat, bone, or the meninges covering the spinal cord. In Meningocele, the meninges protrude through the spine, forming a sac of spinal fluid on the back. Finally, Myelomeningocele stands as the most severe form of spina bifida, involving the exposure of the spinal cord or nerves in a sac through an opening in the spine.

The current medical prevention for the defect is the daily use of 400 micrograms of Folic Acid during pregnancy, and nutritional preventions are also suggested in infant and adult diets. For those living with spina bifida, an elevation in body weight can impact mobility, fostering a sedentary lifestyle that exacerbates the discrepancy between caloric intake and expenditure.

Several elements can influence the nutritional intake of children during their growth, encompassing factors such as brain stem dysfunction, shunt obstruction, silent aspiration, sleep apnea, recurrent infections, and changes in feeding dynamics due to frequent hospitalization and caregiver stress.

Importance of Knowledge Mobilization

Knowledge mobilization are activities that communicate and enable the use of expert research both academic and non-academic, leading to heightened awareness of the subject. Knowledge mobilization essentially links research results in the context of real-world issues thereby showing how research can be used in everyday life. These tools lead to better awareness of the subject, connecting valuable and impactful research within and beyond academia. It is important that this activity be communicated in lay terms and the research is accessible and accurate, for a lay audience to better understand unfamiliar concepts. This vital step allows for maximized intellectual, cultural, social and economic impact. Furthermore, individuals can adapt and use scientific findings within their daily lives.

The Rationale of Team Knowledge Mobilization Resource

Due to its many uses and ease of accessibility, engagement, and inclusivity for individuals of all ages, a website was our first choice for a knowledge mobilization tool. The website functions as a dynamic platform that provides essential information, promotes a user-friendly experience, and can reach a varied audience. Furthermore, the website's adaptability makes it possible to accommodate a variety of learning styles and technological capacities. In addition to drawing visitors, an easily accessible website promotes comprehension of key messages at a deeper level as it influences user attitude and involvement. Therefore, it is crucial to prioritize accessibility. We have gathered data and information in the form of readings, videos, diagrams, images, and graphs that allow browsers to have a variety of different approaches to learn about our topic. This ensures individuals with diverse learning preferences are accommodated, ensuring that they can engage with the content in ways that suit their styles and preferences. We also included a quiz on the website that gives the audience a chance to learn with active recall, which is a significant method to retain knowledge. It has been demonstrated that actively retrieving knowledge from memory through active recall greatly enhances learning results. Research done by Jeffrey. D and Henry L. Roediger, with a group of college students, states that students utilizing active recall techniques were able to retain roughly 80% of the recently introduced terms, whereas those in the control group, who simply reviewed a series of cards, only managed to recall about 34% of the terms. Therefore, we believe that our resources along with the website improve user engagement and understanding of the topic and encourage active participation in learning.

Interactive Component

SpinaFit allows individuals globally who are learning about spina bifida and those who may be diagnosed with spina bifida a platform to educate themselves and learn about what this condition is. Though there will be many interactive components within this website to allow individuals to be active participants such as article links, videos and a Twine link, the main interactive component within this website will be an interactive test. An interactive test offers a dynamic and engaging way to present complex information to a lay audience. Although traditional tests are extremely popular in the 20th century, interactive tests allow users to explore, manipulate and delve deeper into the test bank at their own pace. Within interactive tests, the level of interactivity allows for the user to be engaged by providing a personalized experience for unique individuals dependent on their interests and learning styles. Through embedding multimedia elements within this website through videos, links and quizzes, the user's experience will be enriched. By fostering active participation and enabling users to interact with the content, this interactive test transforms passive viewers into active participants allowing information to be conveyed effectively within SpinaFit.
